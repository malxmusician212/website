Malcolm Lazarow

I spend my time thinking about mathematics (pure and applied), physics, and music. I often give myself challenging goals, and I will report my progress on this website. Below you can find information about my work.

My current goal is to upload simulation applets onto this website to showcase my work ethic and knowledge (it should be noted that, unless otherwise stated, all work uploaded here has been done independently, in my own free time, out of passion and an insatiable thirst for understanding this strange universe). In particular, I am working on simulating the collision of two black holes (as observed by LIGO on September 14, 2015) via numerical 3+1 relativity, as well as numerous problems in statistical mechanics. I am studying advanced quantum mechanics and quantum field theory so that I can upload simulation applets of lattice field theory.

My ultimate goal is to participate in active research in computational physics; in particular, my interest lies in particle physics. I seek to build up my computational skills and physical knowledge to tackle problems in quantum gravity from a computational perspective. About Me/Summary of My Education Thusfar

I was born and raised in the San Francisco Bay Area in 1994. Throughout my entire life, I have been in love with music and scientific explanations of the natural universe. I began playing piano at the age of 5, and started composing soon thereafter. When I was in the 12th grade, I took calculus and fell in love with mathematics.

When deciding what to study in college (I attended Northwestern University), I was unable to choose between mathematics and music composition. To make matters worse, I hadn't the slightest idea what the difference between applied mathematics and pure mathematics was (no one in my family does anything remotely related to math or music, so I grew up believing that high school mathematics exhausted all topics in the field). Therefore, I decided that I would attain two bachelors degrees and three majors: a Bachelor of Science with majors in Engineering Sciences & Applied Mathematics (ESAM) and Pure Mathematics, as well as a Bachelor of Music in Music Composition. During my last few years, I realized the beauty of physics through some courses on electromagnetism and so I "contentrated" my ESAM major in physics.

In some sense, the story of my life is indecisiveness: I love many things and am fascinated by them, so instead of choosing between them, I study all of them and combine. On that note, when I was in my final year at Northwestern, I decided that I wanted to write a thesis, but I could not decide between an ESAM thesis or a Pure Math thesis. Of course, I chose to write both.
